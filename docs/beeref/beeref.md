# BeeRef

For a long time, I've felt like something was missing regarding managing my interests. Sometimes I discover something nice—it could be a video game, an anime, a website, or just an interest in learning to do something. The best I do is acknowledge that I'm interested in revisiting it in the future, but without making it more concrete. I needed something that could remind me later of what it is I'm currently interested in, acting as suggestions to engage in those activities instead of staring blankly at a screen, idle. So, I decided to make a system where I could input my interests as I discover I like them, so I could be reminded of them without needing much, if any, interaction with the system at all for it to work. I decided against text reminders; no matter what kind of algorithm I used to schedule gentle reminders, it would still feel too dry and cerebral, like a set of orders. So, I decided to use images. I would find a nice image for each activity, topic, or site that I liked and arrange it on a canvas, allowing me to move, resize, and organize the images as I see fit. Gemini suggested two programs that would work for this: `pureref` and `beeref`. I picked `beeref` because it's open-source, and I'm glad I did because it works great.

I can just drag and drop images from my browser onto it, and it imports them automatically.

I can resize, move, rotate, and mirror the images, placing them wherever I want.

I can zoom in and out of the canvas and move around; it's basically infinite.

Everything is saved in a single `interest.bee` file, which is currently `2.7MB`.

So now, when I decide I want to "subscribe" to an activity, I just find a nice image to represent it and put it on the canvas somewhere.

And I can leave this open and glance at it from time to time, to slowly and naturally get reminded and excited about things I like or should do. Eventually, when I get the energy and determination, I engage in one of them.

As things add up, I can also use this as a memory tool for images of niche websites, books, or whatever I think I should dig deeper into from time to time. Without this, I might forget a lot of those things; it acts as a set of visual bookmarks.

The `.bee` file can be easily shared, and maybe there are some ways to enable real-time collaboration with multiple people.

Over time, the canvas might get huge, becoming a nice historical monument to my interests over the years.
